<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Contact_Us">Contact Us</span></h2>
<p>We welcome your suggestions, questions, and other feedback at <a rel="nofollow" class="external text" href="mailto:ohc@ohiohistory.org">ohc@ohiohistory.org</a>. To learn more about the Ohio History Connection, please visit <a rel="nofollow" class="external text" href="http://www.ohiohistory.org">www.ohiohistory.org</a>.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Ohio_Quick_Facts">Ohio Quick Facts</span></h2>
<dl><dd>1. Name: From <a href="/w/Iroquois" title="Iroquois" class="mw-redirect">Iroquois</a> word meaning "great river"  </dd></dl>
<dl><dd>2. Statehood: On February 19, 1803 Congress passed an act stating that the citizens of Ohio had adopted a constitution in accordance with the 1802 enabling act and the said state had become one of the United States of America. The <a href="/w/Ohio_General_Assembly" title="Ohio General Assembly">Ohio General Assembly</a> met for the first time in <a href="/w/Chillicothe" title="Chillicothe" class="mw-redirect">Chillicothe</a> on March 1, 1803, the date Ohioans now celebrate as Statehood Day.  </dd></dl>
<dl><dd>3. Capitals
<dl><dd>1. <a href="/w/Chillicothe" title="Chillicothe" class="mw-redirect">Chillicothe</a>: 1803 - 1809</dd>
<dd>2. <a href="/w/Zanesville" title="Zanesville" class="mw-redirect">Zanesville</a>: 1809 - 1812</dd>
<dd>3. <a href="/w/Chillicothe" title="Chillicothe" class="mw-redirect">Chillicothe</a>: 1812 - 1816</dd>
<dd>4. <a href="/w/Columbus" title="Columbus" class="mw-redirect">Columbus</a>: 1816 - present</dd></dl></dd>
<dd>4. Nickname: <a href="/w/Buckeye_State" title="Buckeye State" class="mw-redirect">Buckeye State</a></dd></dl>
<dl><dd>5. Total Population: 11,536,504 (as of 2010 census)</dd></dl>
